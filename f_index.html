My role model: Canan Dağdeviren

  • Background and Early Life
  • Projects and Inventions
  • Awards and Works

    • Dağdeviren was born in 1985 in Istanbul, Turkey. Her interest in science sparked at a young age. Neither of her parents were scientists, but they were supportive of her interests. Currently she is the principal investigator at the MIT Media Lab. She studied physics engineering at Hacettepe University in Ankara and obtained a Master of Science degree at Sabanci University in Istanbul. Later she won a Fullbright scholarship to study in the US and conducted research in Material sciences and engineering at the University of Illinois. Canan Dağdeviren is the first Turkish scientist in history who became a Junior Fellow in the Society of Fellows at Harvard University. She can speal seven different Languages as well.

    Dağdeviren has contributed to the Biotechnology and Bioengineering field. She focuses on the main disciplines of physics, chemistry, medicine, electronics, and mechanical and material sciences. Many of the inventions she works on with her team combine most or all of these disciples, which is shown with the wearable pacemaker and the brain needle.

    The wearable pace maker is flexible and ultra-thin. It is a piezoelectric integrated device. The pacemaker harvests the energy created by the human body to power for it to work more efficiently and is less costly compared to battery powered pacemakers.
    The Brain needle is used for patients with Parkinson's disease. It injects drugs for the treatment of the disease directly into the brain to have a greater affect (does not get filtered through the liver or blood brain barrier compared to normal drugs).
    Canan Dağdeviren won over 30 prestigious awards. She was mentioned in the Forbes Magazine on the "Forbes 30 under 30 in Science"
    She also published 18 research paper and an original patent
